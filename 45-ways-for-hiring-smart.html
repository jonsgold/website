Book Summary
Title 45 Effective Ways for Hiring Smart! How to Predict Winners and Losers in the Incredibly Expensive People-Reading Game
Author Pierre Mornell
Published 1998
ISBN 0898159725
ISBN13 9780898159721
Introduction
  • A mistake in hiring, rectified within 6 months by replacing that employee, costs 2.5 the person's annual salary.
  • Example of Tony Razzano, chief scout for the San Francisco 49ers in the 1980s - he drafted Joe Montana and Jerry Rice.
  • His system was to see 200 plays of the eligible player - watch him in real live situations.
  • The best predictor of future behaviour is past behaviour.
  • The pre-interview strategies are designed to eliminate up to 80% of inappropriate applicants.
Chapter 1: Pre-Interview Strategies
  • Most interviewees prepare for an interview.
  • So should the company script some pre-interview plays for which there can be little preparation by the candidate.
  • A less predictable interview allows to more accurately evaluate performance on the job.
  • Also crucial to evaluate if it will be a good long-term relationship.
Chapter 2: Strategies During the Interview
  • Results of an interview may be misleading, only showing the candidate passed the chemistry and interview tests.
  • A good con artist can fool you every time.
  • Interviews with induced stress don't work - putting a candidate on the defensive will only put up walls and demonstrate his defensive behavior.
Chapter 3: Strategies After the Interview
  • Like in fishing, when hiring excellent people, half the battle remains after the candidate leaves your office. Like hooking the fish but not catching it.
  • If you keep your eyes and ears open during the post-interview phase, you'll learn more about the candidate's behaviour and track record than you ever thought possible.
Chapter 4: Checking References
  • No hire is better than a bad hire.
  • References offer a photo album of strengths and weaknesses.
  • We check references every day: we ask about good babysitters, mechanics, etc.
  • When it comes to hiring, we balk, not wanting to hear negative information (especially if we like the candidate).
  • When we do, we confirm facts instead of asking for information.
  • Be persistent, eventually you will discover something. Ask references for references.
Chapter 5: Final Strategies
  • All the hiring strategies were designed to save time, but they initially take time.
